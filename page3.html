Academic Facilities

Art Annex

Located adjacent to Southplex housing, provides space for ceramics, painting, drawing, and sculpture classes.

Coward-Martin Hall

Completed in 2012, houses five spacious classrooms, seven offices, and the Curriculum Library. Equipped with electronic access and twelve computers for student use. Houses the School of Education.

Fisher-Washburn Hall

Constructed in 2002, includes a tiered auditorium, computer laboratory, conference room, spacious classrooms, and faculty offices. Approximately 44 different classes meet weekly. Houses Language and Literature, Social and Behavioral Sciences, Biblical Studies, and School of Business departments.

Garrett Hall of Fine Arts

Located next to Guyton Library, houses the performing arts auditorium and Department of Fine Arts. Provides classrooms, music practice rooms, and faculty offices.

Garrett Annex

Provides spacious room for instrumental and vocal ensembles.

Guyton Library

Provides access to books, periodicals, electronic information, and learning resources. Features thirteen laptops for checkout, a museum of Chinese artifacts, and Alumni archives including a doll collection.

Health Annex

Located in the rear of the Wellness Center, houses the Kinesiology department with three faculty offices, a classroom, and a classroom/laboratory.

Lowrey Administration Building

Constructed in the late 1920's with ongoing renovations. Houses the Department of Mathematics and Natural Sciences, administrative offices, and a 773-seat auditorium. The Teaching and Learning Center (TLC) provides 28 desktop computers and tutoring areas.

Lowrey Church, Newcomb Room

Located on the ground level of the historic Lowrey Memorial Baptist Church building built in 1908. The renovated Newcomb Room serves as the classroom for the BMCU Chorale.

Nursing Annex

2022 renovated space connected to the Lowrey Memorial Baptist Church. Features 4 state-of-the-art simulation rooms, skills lab, two large classrooms, and seven offices for nursing administration and faculty.

Wilfred C. Tyler Gymnasium

Houses one classroom, three faculty offices, and meeting areas for physical education classes and activities.
